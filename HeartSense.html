HeartSense

No more lonely hearts.

Interaction Design | Experience Design | Physical Prototyping

Project Team | Aditya Anupam | Thanawit Tae Prasongpongchai | Shubhangi Gupta | Charlie Denton | Olivia Cox | Da-In Ryoo

Introduction

Cardiographs often depict the human heart in an isolated way. It helps medical professionals diagnose us, but it offers little in terms of understanding our own hearts. The heart is a social and sensitive organ, and it can be affected by other people an envionmental stimuli. Because of this, the goal of this research is to create a visualization which shows how the heart behaves in collective experiences.

This project is a continuation of previous work done by Dr. Nassim Parvin of Georgia Tech and other researchers within her research lab, the Design & Social Interaction Studio. Documentation of previous iterations of HeartSense can be found here.

Trails and Memory

My iteration of the project will take the form of a table where participants will collaboratively use their biometrics to create a projected visualization of their collective experience as they listen to music. Each participant will get one quadrant of a circle of LEDs that will pulse along with their heart rate. As time passes on, their heart rate patterns will be passed on to the next inner ring, visualizing the change over time.

The brightness of the LEDs decreases with each ring.

Building and Prototyping

We used Arduino pulse sensors to capture heart rate data and a WS2812B LED strip for our lights. Since we are currently working on this project, we are striving towards adding the other two rings, using four Arduino boards to support up to four participants, and covering the LEDs with fabric so that the lights will diffuse.

In the Works

The final form of HeartSense will have the three rings covered with fabric flowers to create an aesthetic experience about the interactions of embodiment, memory, and shared spaces.
